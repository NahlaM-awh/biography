Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam – Biography

Basic Information

  • Full Name: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam
  • Born: October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • Died: July 27, 2015, in Shillong, Meghalaya
  • Known As: Missile Man of India, People’s President
  • Profession: Scientist, Author, Teacher, President of India

Early Life

Dr. Kalam came from a simple family and loved science from a young age. He studied physics and aerospace engineering.

Career as a Scientist

He worked in ISRO and DRDO, leading projects like SLV-III and missile programs (Agni, Prithvi). He was also part of the 1998 nuclear tests.

President of India (2002–2007)

Kalam became the 11th President of India. He was widely loved and often spoke to students, earning the title “People’s President.”

After Presidency

He became a teacher and continued to inspire students. He passed away while giving a lecture at IIM Shillong.
